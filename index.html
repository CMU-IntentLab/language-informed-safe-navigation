Updating Robot Safety Representations Online from Natural Language Feedback

Leonardo Santos*, Zirui Li*, Lasse Peters , Somil Bansal† , Andrea Bajcsy†
*Indicates Equal Contribution †Indicates Equal Advising
arXiv Code

Abstract

Robots must operate safely when deployed in novel and human-centered environments, like the home. Current safe control approaches predominantly assume that the robot knows a priori the relevant safety constraints, and thus the robot can pre-compute a corresponding safety controller. While this may make sense for some types of safety constraints (e.g., by looking at a floor plan, the robot can know where the walls of a room are and can compute a safety controller to never collide), other aspects of safety are inherently personal and semantic, and can only be detected at deployment time when the robot is interacting in a specific environment and with a specific person (e.g., fragile objects or expensive rugs). In this work, we use human language feedback coupled with the robot’s high-dimensional observations (e.g., RGB-D data) to update the robot’s representation of safety constraints. Once the new constraints are inferred, we update the robot’s Hamilton-Jacobi reachability-based safety controller online via efficient warm-starting updates.
